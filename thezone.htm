"Oh crap we're in the zone!!"

The zone is an area of speed between about 1.0 - .85 . It's the only speed you actually get sick in. Below it there isn't enough speed to have an effect. Above it your whole brain just kind of looses track of everything else and you just go into your own world.

When I first went fast I felt an interesting thing unrelated to the zone but I thought was note worthy. The effect of the Gs pulling you backward makes the appearance of the entire world rotating forwards so you feel like you and the cup and everything else is pointing up like you are leaning back. After a while of making runs I stopped noticing this.

While in the zone, life is excruciatingly painful. It WILL be the most excruciating 90 seconds of your life. We try very very hard to stay out of this speed by accelerating through it quickly. If we are too tired to get passed the zone we will stop immediately. Only one time can I remember not stopping the run. Me and Eric were hanging out at the cups (like there is a time when we aren't) and two little girls (`bout 12 - 13 yrs old) came up to us and asked if we would take them on a run. And we really can't say no to our fans. So after about 4 runs so far that night, with Erics hand ripped open, and me being pretty tired, we took them on a run. 90 seconds in the zone. The worst run ever done by TTC. The worst part of it is we don't often get fans coming up to us and ask us for a ride. We hadn't had anyone ask us in months, and when we finally did we spent it all in the zone. They thought it was the fastest anyone had ever gone before and loved every minute of it, but we were in intense pain. Only about 4 times has any member of TTC thrown up after a run. Usually we feel better after a run (no one actually believes that but it is true). But on account of the zone I have thrown up 4 times myself. I did that night.
