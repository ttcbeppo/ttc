Breathing

No matter what, your muscles need oxygen. They simply won't move without it. The best way to get it is to breath. But breathing in a high G environment is extremely difficult. Under high Gs blood rushes away from the center of the cup squeezing your lungs making it very difficult to inhale. The technique that usually work best for us is to breath like a fighter pilot. While the method we use was developed for positive G applications, where it keeps O2 in your brain while the blood runs away from your head, it works well for our application because of our high energy expenditure. It keeps blood near our muscles the same way it keeps blood near a fighter pilots brain. Our technique is to pretty much to hyperventilate before we pull. That gets as much O2 in our blood stream as we can. We breath as deep and as fast as we can and compress the air in our lungs and breath out as fast as we can. It is a very violent breathing method and shouldn't be done for any length of time while you aren't pulling Gs as you will quickly pass out, but it seems to help our longevity on a run. When we get in and pull we slow down the aggressiveness of our breathing rate to help us concentrate on how hard we are pulling so we don't rip open. Ripping open sucks. This breathing technique is best for our no pacing, one at a time, pull as hard as physically possible pulling technique. It may not be the best method for your pulling technique though. Because of the high Gs some method is needed. Most people we take on rides complain they cant breath. It's the main reason people tell us to stop. It's also the #1 reason team members ask us to stop pulling. That or we're too tired to get out of the zone 'cause we made too many runs in one night.
