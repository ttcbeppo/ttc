G forces: the reason fast is fast

Without the severe G forces we would be able to go much faster than we do. Anyone can deal with the spinning and not get dizzy or sick, as long as your past the zone. But it gets real hard to pull when the Gs mount up. First reason is you cant get in and pull. At a .65 the Gs pulling you outward are up to 5, which means your torso weighs 600 lbs. Depending on which method you use this can put a real damper in your ability to move forward to get in. Brian M uses a technique where he leans against the back of the cup. This way he has alot less effect of the Gs. But he has really long arms, so we cant all use that method. But gs ripping you toward the outside is the biggest problem we face toward going fast.

Perhaps the worst limitation Gs provide, is the added blood pressure toward the rear of your body, which is the center of your back. This provides a large portion of force squeezing in your lungs, making it very hard to breath. One of the most common reasons we stop a run, is someone says they cant breath. We have pretty much adopted the breathing methods of fighter pilots, to counter act this. Read the page on breathing for more on this.

Another limiting factor, is the fear of falling out. Falling out flat out sucks. I've done it myself, and it can get pretty hairy if you aren't careful. When your not pulling "grab the lip". Reach down between your legs and grab the edge of the seat and don't let go. We ALWAYS make sure we "have a lip" before the run starts. If we don't it can get a little hairy sometimes, when we start to slip out. One bad thing about gs is "redeye". This is a phenomenon that happens when blood is forced into the eyes from excessive blood pressure. It pretty much only happens from Gs. You don't see it with SCUBA divers because the pressure is everywhere while with Gs it is only around the edges and back and not the front.
